About Me

My name is Shesh Narayan Dhurandhar, and I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at IIT Madras.

Exploring fluid dynamics from the molecular to the continuum scale.

I received my Ph.D. in Mechanical and Industrial Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee, where I specialized in rarefied gas dynamics and radiative heat transfer.

My research focuses on advancing the understanding of high-speed and hypersonic flows through computational fluid dynamics (CFD), with applications in rarefied gas dynamics, radiative heat transfer, multiphase flow dynamics, and vortex-dominated compressible flows.

I am also actively involved in high-performance computing (MPI, OpenACC, OpenMP) to enable large-scale, high-fidelity flow simulations. I am passionate about developing robust computational models that bridge fundamental fluid dynamics with real-world aerospace and energy applications.

Research Interests & Expertise

  • Rarefied gas dynamics, including hypersonic and low-density flows.
  • High-fidelity CFD simulations using DSMC, OpenFOAM, and custom solvers.
  • Development of non-equilibrium chemical reaction models for high-temperature shock layers.
  • Energy deposition in supersonic flows and advanced radiative heat transfer modeling.
  • Multiphase flow modeling with six-equation formulations and droplet-laden compressible flows.
  • Parallel computing, MPI-based solver development, OpenACC GPU acceleration, and Linux-based HPC environments.

Postdoctoral Researcher

Indian Institute of Technology Madras, India (2021 – Present)

  • Vogel–Escudier Flow & Rayleigh–Bénard Convection: Investigated axial vortex dynamics in a rotating closed cylinder, analyzing compressibility and rarefaction effects. Extended OpenFOAM solvers to incorporate non-continuum corrections and performed parameter studies on vortex breakdown behavior relevant to confined vortex-dominated flows and rotating machinery.
  • Explored buoyancy-driven thermal instabilities in incompressible cylindrical cavities and their interplay with vortex dynamics.
  • Multiphase Flow Past a Cylinder: Developed and parallelized a Fortran-based six-equation multiphase solver with OpenACC and MPI for large-scale GPU-accelerated simulations. Analyzed wake dynamics, drag/lift behavior, and Strouhal number variations, achieving significant computational performance gains.

Ph.D. Research Scholar

Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee, India (2014 – 2020)

  • Coupled DSMC/Quantum-Kinetics Model: Utilized dsmcFoam (OpenFOAM) to study non-equilibrium effects in hypersonic shock layers. Applied quantum-kinetics to chemical reactions, providing improved understanding of Martian atmospheric entry conditions.
  • Radiative Heat Transfer Modeling: Implemented Photon Monte Carlo, spherical harmonics, zonal, and discrete ordinate methods to study gray and non-gray gases. Incorporated coal and ash radiative properties (via Mie theory) and Planck-averaged intensity distributions to enhance fidelity of radiative transfer predictions.

Currently, my research at IIT Madras focuses on analyzing axial vortex dynamics in compressible and rarefied regimes, with applications to aerospace propulsion and rotating machinery. Alongside research, I have mentored undergraduate and graduate students and contributed to teaching initiatives through India’s NPTEL program.

I am excited about advancing aerospace and energy applications, including hypersonic flight, fusion and fission energy systems, and multiphysics high-performance simulations. I welcome discussions on collaborative research opportunities.
