About Me

My name is Shesh Narayan Dhurandhar, and I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at IIT Madras. I completed my Ph.D. from the Department of Mechanical and Industrial Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee, specializing in rarefied gas dynamics. My research focuses on computational fluid dynamics (CFD), high-speed flows, radiative heat transfer, multiphase flow, and hypersonic rarefied gas dynamics.

Research Interests & Expertise

  • Rarefied gas dynamics, including hypersonic and low-density flows.
  • CFD simulations using DSMC, OpenFOAM, and high-performance computing.
  • Development of non-equilibrium chemical reaction models for high-temperature shock layers.
  • Analysis of energy deposition in supersonic flow and radiative heat transfer modeling.
  • Post-processing and data analysis using Tecplot, Origin, and Paraview.
  • Parallel computing, MPI-based code development, OpenACC and Linux-based HPC environments.

Currently, at IIT Madras, I am investigating axial vortex dynamics in wall-bounded compressible flows using DSMC and Navier-Stokes-Fourier approaches. My work aims to analyze vortex topology changes due to rarefaction and compressibility effects. In addition to research, I have mentored undergraduate and graduate students and contributed to academic courses under India's NPTEL program.

I am excited about exploring new areas in aerospace and energy research, including fusion and fission energy applications. Feel free to connect with me to discuss research collaborations or opportunities.
